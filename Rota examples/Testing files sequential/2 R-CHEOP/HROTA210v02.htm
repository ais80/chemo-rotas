Tests

Hepatitis serology (including Hep B sAg and cAb) MUST be tested prior to starting rituximab. This should be completed within the last 3 months of commencing treatment.

NB this is NOT included in 'Blood Borne Virus screen' request.

 

Validity of results for neutrophils and platelets = 96 hours

For bilirubin and ALT = 7 days

Precautions

 

Rituximab Administration Advice

 

First infusion:

Initial rate of 50mg/hr for the first 30 minutes. Can then be escalated in 50mg/hr increments every 30 minutes, to a maximum rate of 400mg/hr.

 

Subsequent infusions:

If first infusion is well tolerated, the following rapid schedule can be used: give 100ml over 30 minutes. Then give remaining 400ml over 60 minutes.

In patients receiving the rapid infusion schedule, record all infusional toxicity on the appropriate form.

In the event of a slower infusion rate being required, use the following schedule: Initial rate of 100mg/hr for the first 30 minutes. Can then be escalated in 100mg/hr increments every 30 minutes, to a maximum rate of 400mg/hr.

 

Fast infusion rate for patients who tolerate their first cycle – 20% of total dose given over 30 minutes and 80% of total dose over the following 60 minutes.

 

Elderly patients or those with a high tumour burden may require a slower infusion rate.

If a patient develops severe cytokine release syndrome the infusion should be interrupted immediately. On resolution, the infusion can be resumed at not more than one-half the previous rate. Mild to moderate infusion-related reactions usually respond to a reduction in infusion rate.

 

During infusion, the patient’s vital signs (BP, pulse, respiration and temperature) should be monitored every 15 minutes for the first hour, and then if stable, hourly until infusion stops.
