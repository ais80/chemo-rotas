Tests

Hepatitis serology (including Hep B sAg and cAb) MUST be tested prior to starting rituximab. This should be completed within the last 3 months of commencing treatment.

NB this is NOT included in 'Blood Borne Virus screen' request.

 

This rota features potassium chloride. If potassium is 6.0 or greater or there is a significant change please contact prescriber.

Precautions

Allopurinol Allopurinol 100mg od in patients with renal impairment.

Rasburicase Consider rasburicase for patients at high risk of tumour lysis syndrome.

Filgrastim* - To be given daily from day 13 until neutrophils > 1 x 109/l.

Co-trimoxazole 480mg bd po MWF (to stop 7 days pre-MTX and restart 5 days post-MTX). Continues until 6 months post end of chemotherapy.         

 

 

Two cycles each of alternating R-CODOX-M and R-IVAC will be given followed by two further doses of Rituximab (on days 21 and 42 of the 2nd

IVAC schedule).

Start cycle 2 of  R-CODOX-M on first day after R-IVAC.

 

Rituximab

Administration advice

First infusion:                        Initial rate of 50mg/hr for the first 30 minutes.  Can then be escalated in 50mg/hr increments every 30 minutes, to a maximum rate of 400mg/hr.

Subsequent infusions:         If first infusion well tolerated, the following rapid schedule can be used:  Give 100mls over 30 minutes.  Then give remaining 400mls over 60 minutes.  In patients receiving the rapid infusion schedule, record all infusional toxicity on the appropriate form.

                                                                                In the event of a slower infusion rate being required, use the following schedule:  Initial rate of 100mg/hr for the first 30 minutes.  Can then be escalated in 100mg/hr increments every 30 minutes, to a maximum rate of 400mg/hr.

                                               

     Elderly patients or those with a high tumour burden may require a slower infusion rate.

     If a patient develops severe cytokine release syndrome the infusion should be interrupted immediately. On resolution, the infusion can be resumed at not more than one-half the previous rate.  Mild to moderate infusion-related reactions usually respond to a reduction in infusion rate.

     During infusion, the patients vital signs (bp, pulse, respiration and temperature) should be monitored every 30 minutes until infusion stops.
